Perishable Demand Predictor

Python XGBoost Streamlit Google Gemini AI Business Intelligence

Overview

FrostMart UK faces a critical challenge — managing perishable goods efficiently. This project delivers an end‑to‑end machine learning and data‑driven solution that predicts weekly product demand, minimizes waste, and empowers procurement teams with actionable recommendations.

The Core Problem: An estimated annual financial loss of £12.2M due to product wastage and overstocking, representing approximately 4.77% of total revenue.

Key Features

  • XGBoost Regressor: High-accuracy demand forecasting (R² 0.9959).
  • Interactive Dashboard: Streamlit app with tabs for single product, batch, and model insights.
  • AI Chat Assistant: Integrated Google Gemini chatbot for querying sales and wastage data.
  • Automated Procurement: Human-readable order recommendations with buffer suggestions.
  • Batch Processing: Capability to upload CSVs for bulk forecasting.

Model Performance & Evaluation

We evaluated multiple algorithms to find the optimal balance between speed and accuracy. The Gradient Boosting Regressor (XGBoost) emerged as the superior model.

Model R² Score RMSE MAE MAPE
Linear Regression 0.63 21.5 19.8 5.4%
Random Forest 0.82 15.3 12.7 3.2%
Gradient Boosting 0.9959 75.28 16.27 0.86%

FrostMart UK: Business Insights Report

Prepared by: Francis Afful Gyan | Date: November 2025

1. Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of FrostMart UK's operational and financial performance. While the company demonstrates robust market presence with an estimated annual revenue of £255.7M, operational inefficiencies in perishable goods management result in an annual loss of £12.2M.

The implementation of our new predictive model is projected to reduce waste-related losses by 30-40% and unlock a potential revenue uplift of 10-20%.

2. Product & Regional Performance

Dairy: Top performer. "Mozzarella 250g" sold over 2.09 million units.
Bakery: Highest wastage rate (14.7%). Critical target for intervention.
Meat: Highest average price (£7.27). Low volume wastage but high financial impact.
Beverages: Longest shelf life (216 days). Optimization focus is on capital efficiency, not spoilage.

3. Regional Analysis

London: Highest sales volume but disproportionately high wastage. This region is the priority for the rollout of new inventory tools.
North West & Scotland: Top-performing regions with lower wastage. Operational best practices from here should be scaled nationwide.

4. Strategic Recommendations

Recommendation 1: Launch Targeted Wastage Reduction

Mandatory use of the AI forecasting tool for the Bakery category and all London stores.
Target: Reduce Bakery wastage from 14.7% to <10% in 6 months.

Recommendation 2: Dynamic Pricing System

Leverage the tool to identify expiring products and auto-suggest a 25% discount.
Target: Increase sell-through rate of near-expiry items by 30%.

Recommendation 3: Optimize Marketing Spend

Reallocate budget based on model-identified ROI periods (e.g., January).
Target: Improve marketing correlation to 0.15 within 12 months.

Recommendation 4: Full-Scale AI Adoption

Comprehensive training for store managers on the Streamlit dashboard.
Target: Achieve 95% adoption rate across all UK stores in 6 months.

5. Conclusion

FrostMart UK is at a pivotal juncture. The annual loss of £12.2M is a formidable challenge but also our single greatest opportunity for value creation. By adopting this data-centric strategy and deploying the high-accuracy predictive model, FrostMart can transition from reactive decision-making to a proactive, intelligent operation, solidifying its competitive advantage.
